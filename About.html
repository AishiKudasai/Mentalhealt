Understanding Mental Health

Common Mental Health Conditions

Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety disorders involve excessive fear or anxiety that affects daily functioning. This includes generalized anxiety disorder, panic disorder, and phobias.

Mood Disorders

These include depression and bipolar disorder, which involve persistent feelings of sadness or extreme mood fluctuations.

Schizophrenia

A severe mental disorder affecting how a person thinks, feels, and behaves, often involving psychosis and difficulty distinguishing reality.

Eating Disorders

Conditions like anorexia nervosa, bulimia nervosa, and binge-eating disorder involve extreme emotions and behaviors surrounding weight and food.

Signs and Symptoms to Watch For

Emotional Symptoms

+

Feeling sad or down for extended periods, excessive fears or worries, extreme feelings of guilt or worthlessness, sudden mood swings or irritability, withdrawal from friends and activities, loss of interest in hobbies, significant tiredness, and low energy.

Physical Symptoms

+

Unexplained aches and pains, significant changes in eating habits or sex drive, difficulty sleeping or sleeping too much, detachment from reality (delusions), paranoia or hallucinations, inability to cope with daily problems or stress.

Behavioral Symptoms

+

Withdrawal from friends and activities, significant tiredness, low energy or problems sleeping, confusion, reduced ability to concentrate, alcohol or drug abuse, major changes in eating habits, excessive anger or violence.

Mental Health vs. Mental Illness

Mental Health

Mental health refers to our cognitive, behavioral, and emotional wellbeing - how we think, feel, and behave. Everyone has mental health, whether or not they have a mental illness.

  • Ability to enjoy life
  • Resilience to adversity
  • Balanced lifestyle
  • Flexibility and adaptability
  • Self-actualization

Mental Illness

Mental illness refers to diagnosed conditions that affect a person's thinking, feeling, mood, or behavior and interfere with daily functioning.

  • Clinical depression
  • Anxiety disorders
  • Bipolar disorder
  • Schizophrenia
  • Eating disorders
  • Addictive behaviors

Self-Care Toolkit

Daily Practices for Mental Wellness

  • Mindfulness: Start with 5 minutes of breathing exercises each morning.

  • Movement: Incorporate 30 minutes of physical activity you enjoy.

  • Journaling: Write down three things you're grateful for each day.

  • Sleep: Maintain a consistent sleep schedule for better mental health.
